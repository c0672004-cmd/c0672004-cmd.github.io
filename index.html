🏅 Olympic Success: A Statistical Study

A multivariate research project analyzing how national wealth (GDP) and population dynamics influence athletic performance. This study uses a combination of descriptive statistics and regression modeling to identify the key drivers of international sporting success.

  • Key Findings: National GDP shows a strong positive correlation with medal counts (r = 0.769), while population size surprisingly exhibits a negative relationship in predictive models, suggesting that resource allocation is more critical than sheer size.
  • Advanced Metrics: Developed a custom "Efficiency Index" to highlight nations like Dominica and Jamaica that over-perform relative to their economic base.
  • Statistical Rigor: Performed ANOVA for regional comparisons and VIF checks to ensure the reliability of the regression coefficients.

Tools: R, Tidyverse, ANOVA, VIF Analysis, ggplot2, Hypothesis Testing

🍷 Wine Quality Prediction: Python Analysis

Implemented a Gradient Descent optimizer from scratch in Python to predict red wine quality. This study identifies non-linear relationships within physicochemical data by performing a comparative analysis between Multivariate Linear and Polynomial regression models.

  • Key Results: Demonstrated that wine quality is intrinsically non-linear; a Degree 2 Polynomial model significantly increased predictive power (R2 = 0.428) compared to the baseline linear approach.
  • Primary Drivers: Alcohol content and Volatile Acidity were statistically identified as the most influential factors in quality perception.

Tools: Python, NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Gradient Descent, Statistical Modeling

🤖 MNIST Digit Classification: Machine Learning & Deep Learning

A comparative study of five machine learning algorithms—Logistic Regression, LDA, Decision Trees, Random Forest, and Neural Networks—to classify handwritten digits from the MNIST dataset. The project focuses on balancing computational efficiency with predictive accuracy.

  • Model Optimization: Achieved a high-performance **99.17% test accuracy** by optimizing a CNN (modified LeNet-5 architecture) with Batch Normalization, ReLU activations, and Dropout layers.
  • Advanced Techniques: Implemented Data Augmentation (rotation, zoom, shifting) and a variable learning rate scheduler to improve model robustness and convergence.
  • Comparative Analysis: Conducted 5-fold cross-validation across all baseline models to evaluate the trade-offs between linear simplicity and non-linear complexity.
